TIMELINE

Born and Upbringing

( 1967-1985 )

Satya Nadella, CEO of Microsoft, was born in Hyderabad, India, in 1967. He pursued his passion for technology, obtaining a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering and later completing a master's degree in computer science and an MBA. Nadella's upbringing and education paved the way for his successful career as Microsoft's CEO.

Academic Life

( 1985-2002 )

Satya Nadella's academic life was marked by a passion for technology and a dedication to learning. He earned a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering and went on to pursue a master's degree in computer science. Nadella further expanded his knowledge and skills by completing an MBA. His academic journey equipped him with the expertise and foundation necessary to excel in his career as the CEO of Microsoft.

Initial Jobs

( 1987-1990 )

After completing his education, Satya Nadella started his career at Sun Microsystems, contributing to the UNIX operating system. He later joined Microsoft in 1992 and played a crucial role in the development of their cloud services. Nadella's impactful work led to his appointment as the Executive Vice President of Cloud and Enterprise, overseeing the success of Azure. His initial jobs showcased his technological expertise and strategic vision.

Joined Microsoft

( 1992-2014 )

Satya Nadella joined Microsoft in 1992 and rose through the ranks to become the CEO in 2014. His leadership brought a strong focus on cloud services, leading to the success of Microsoft Azure. Nadella introduced innovative products like Office 365 and Microsoft Teams, driving the company's growth and fostering technological advancements. He prioritizes transformative technologies and empowering people through technology.

CEO Microsoft

( 2014-Present )

After becoming CEO of Microsoft, Satya Nadella shifted the company's focus to cloud computing, leading to the development of the successful Azure platform. He embraced open-source technologies and revitalized the Windows brand with Windows 10. Nadella oversaw the acquisition of LinkedIn and expanded Microsoft's hardware offerings. He also promoted diversity and inclusion within the company and authored the book "Hit Refresh" to share his vision for the future.

Awards and Achiements

Satya Nadella has received several prestigious awards and achievements, including being named the Financial Times Person of the Year in 2019 and receiving the India Today Global Indian of the Year award in 2020. He was recognized as Fortune's Businessperson of the Year in 2019 and was included in CNBC's Top 25 Business Leaders of the Last 25 Years. Nadella has also been honored as Glassdoor's Highest Rated CEO and received the Padma Bhushan, one of India's highest civilian awards, in 2020.
